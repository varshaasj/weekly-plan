The mid-task brain drift — here's what's actually happening
Your brain generates new thoughts constantly and treats them as equally urgent. This isn't laziness — it's how some brains are wired. The fix isn't willpower. It's a physical anchor: keep a sticky note next to you. Every intrusive thought goes on the note. Then you return to the current task. The note is your brain's "I'll handle it later" safety valve.
